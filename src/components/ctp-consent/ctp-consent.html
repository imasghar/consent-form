Informed Consent - Application Of Skin Substitute

This is an informed consent document to help you learn about the Skin Substitute Application (Allograft / Xenograft) and the wound. This document will outline the risks and other aspects of the Skin Substitute Application. It is important that you read the whole document carefully, discuss and ask all your questions and concerns with your Wound Care Medical Provider.

INFORMATION / USE OF SKIN SUBSTITUTE

Skin substitute is a biologic dressing derived from humans or animals. It is used to assist in wound healing for chronic wounds or wounds that failed to show significant progress towards closure with standard wound care within the last 4 to 8 weeks. Skin substitutes constitute biologic covers for refractory wounds with full-thickness skin loss, 3rd degree burns, or other disease processes such as pressure ulcers, diabetic neuropathic ulcers, and the skin loss associated with venous insufficiency and other chronic wounds.The production of these biologic skin substitutes or CTPs varies by company and product, but generally involves the creation of immunologically inert biological products containing protein, hormones or enzymes seeded into a matrix which may provide protein or growth factors proposed to stimulate or facilitate healing or promote epithelization.A variety of biosynthetic and tissue-engineered skin substitution products marketed as Human Skin Equivalents (HSE) or Cellular or Tissue-based Products (CTP) are manufactured under an array of trade names and marketed for a variety of indications. All are procured, produced, manufactured, processed and promoted in sufficiently different manners to preclude direct product comparison for equivalency or superiority in randomized controlled trials.

Autografts / Tissue Cultured Autografts -

include the harvest or application of an autologous skin graft.

Skin Substitute Grafts -

include non-autologous human cellular and tissue products (e.g., dermal or epidermal, cellular and acellular, homograft or allograft), non-human cellular or tissue products (i.e., Xenograft, derived from animals such as Pig, Cow, Horse, Sheep, Fish etc.), and biological products (synthetic or xenogeneic) that are applied in a sheet over an open wound to augment wound closure or skin growth.

Potential Risks
  • I have had explained to me in connection with the proposed procedure/treatment:
    1. Wound Infection – May occur, requiring oral, intravenous, or topical antibiotics and further treatments.
    2. Scar tissue formation – Upon 100% wound closure, skin color may be lighter or darker than the rest of the skin. These changes can be permanent.
    3. Re-opening of wound with re-injury – Upon 100% closure of the wound, the affected area may re-open due to pressure, shear, friction, or re-injury due to decreased skin tensile strength.
